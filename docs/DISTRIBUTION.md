# Distribution Strategy

## Channels

### App Store (iOS)

**Primary Distribution:** iOS App Store (Android to follow based on demand)

**App Store Optimization (ASO):**
- **App Name:** IFQ - Quit Vaping & Smoking
- **Subtitle:** Your AI Coach to Quit Nicotine
- **Keywords:** quit vaping, stop smoking, nicotine addiction, quit smoking app, vape quit, cessation coach, stop vaping
- **Category:** Primary: Health & Fitness, Secondary: Lifestyle

**Visual Assets:**
- Screenshots showcasing Vera conversations, progress tracking, milestone celebrations
- Preview video: 15-30 second emotional hook showing the quit journey
- Icon: Bold IFQ mark in Ink + Emerald

**Ratings & Reviews Strategy:**
- Prompt for review at positive moments (milestone celebrations, streak achievements)
- Never prompt during cravings or difficult moments
- Respond to all reviews—especially negative ones—with the IFQ voice (supportive, never defensive)

**Localisation Priority:**
1. US (largest market, highest LTV potential)
2. UK (cultural fit, English-speaking)
3. Australia (high vaping rates, English-speaking)
4. Canada (regulatory-friendly, English-speaking)

### Web (Landing Page)

**Purpose:** Capture intent from social traffic, build email list, establish credibility

**URL:** ifq.app or ifuckingquit.com (redirect to App Store)

**Landing Page Structure:**
1. Hero: Bold headline + "Get the App" CTA
2. The Problem: Quick stats on nicotine addiction
3. Meet Vera: Introduction to the AI coach
4. How It Works: 3-step visual walkthrough
5. Social Proof: Testimonials, success metrics
6. Pricing: Clear subscription tiers
7. FAQ: Address objections
8. Final CTA: Download now

**SEO Strategy:**
- Target high-intent keywords: "how to quit vaping", "quit nicotine app", "stop vaping help"
- Blog content (later phase): Science-backed articles on quitting, myth-busting, success stories
- Focus on featured snippets for quit-related queries

## Marketing

### Content Strategy

**Content Pillars:**

1. **Raw Quit Stories** (40%)
   - Real user journeys (anonymised or with permission)
   - Day-by-day content: "Day 3 is the hardest. Here's why."
   - Before/during/after emotional moments
   - Format: Short-form video, carousel posts

2. **Science Made Interesting** (25%)
   - What nicotine actually does to your brain
   - Why cravings only last 3-5 minutes
   - The timeline of healing after you quit
   - Format: Infographics, explainer videos, duets with misinformation

3. **Industry Exposé** (20%)
   - How vape companies target young people
   - Marketing tactics of Big Tobacco
   - The economics of addiction
   - Format: Stitches, reaction content, documentary-style

4. **Wins & Milestones** (15%)
   - User celebrations (with permission)
   - Money saved calculators
   - Health improvements timeline
   - Format: Achievement graphics, user-generated content

**Content Voice:**
- Match the brand: direct, warm, irreverent, never preachy
- Swear when it adds punch (platform-dependent)
- First-person plural: "we" are in this fight together
- Never shame smokers/vapers—always punch up at the industry

### Social Media

#### Primary Channels (80% of effort)

**TikTok** — Main growth engine
- **Why:** Highest organic reach, perfect demographic fit (18-35), competitors proving the channel
- **Content Types:**
  - POV quit journey videos
  - Craving survival tips (under 60 seconds)
  - Stitch/duet with vaping content
  - "Things nobody tells you about quitting" series
  - Vera "conversations" (simulated coach interactions)
- **Posting Cadence:** 1-2x daily during growth phase
- **Hashtags:** #quitvaping #quittok #nicotinefree #vapefree #quitnicotine
- **Goals:** Build community, drive app downloads, establish brand voice

**Instagram** — Brand building + community
- **Why:** Slightly older demo (25-40), strong for community, visual brand showcase
- **Content Types:**
  - Reels: Repurposed TikTok content
  - Carousels: Science explainers, quit tips, myth-busting
  - Stories: Daily engagement, polls, Q&As, user shoutouts
  - Feed: High-quality milestone graphics, testimonials
- **Posting Cadence:** 1 Reel daily, 3-4 Stories daily, 2-3 feed posts weekly
- **Goals:** Deepen engagement, build email list, retargeting audience

#### Experimental Channels (20% of effort)

**YouTube** — Long-form authority
- **Why:** SEO value, evergreen content, higher intent viewers
- **Content Types:**
  - "The Complete Guide to Quitting Vaping" (pillar content)
  - Documentary-style quit journeys
  - Science deep-dives (10-15 min)
  - Shorts: Repurposed TikTok content
- **Posting Cadence:** 1 long-form monthly, 3-5 Shorts weekly
- **Goals:** SEO capture, establish expertise, YouTube → App funnel

**Twitter/X** — Thought leadership + virality
- **Why:** Potential for viral moments, health/tech audience, founder-led growth
- **Content Types:**
  - Threads on addiction science
  - Hot takes on nicotine industry news
  - User success story threads
  - Behind-the-scenes of building IFQ
- **Posting Cadence:** 1-2x daily
- **Goals:** Build founder profile, capture viral moments, B2B connections

#### Proactive Engagement Strategy

**Philosophy:** Don't wait for people to find us—show up where quitting conversations are already happening.

**Daily Engagement Routine:**
- Search hashtags: #quitvaping, #quittingnicotine, #vapefree, #dayoneagain, #smokefree
- Find posts from people sharing their quit journey (day counts, struggles, wins)
- Engage authentically—celebrate their wins, offer support during struggles
- Comment on other cessation influencers' content (build relationships, not competition)
- Respond to questions about quitting even when IFQ isn't mentioned

**Engagement Voice:**
- Supportive, never salesy
- Celebrate specifics: "Day 12 is no joke—you're past the hardest part"
- Offer genuine tips without pushing the app
- Only mention IFQ if directly relevant and helpful
- Be the account people recognise as "actually helpful"

**Targets:**
- 20-30 meaningful comments/engagements per day per platform
- Build relationships with 5-10 other creators in the cessation space
- Track engagement-to-follow conversion

**Why This Works:**
- Puts IFQ in front of high-intent users at the moment they're thinking about quitting
- Builds brand recognition through helpfulness, not ads
- Creates goodwill with other creators who may become partners
- Algorithm rewards accounts that engage, not just post

#### Community Channels (Organic seeding)

**Reddit**
- Participate authentically in r/QuitVaping, r/stopsmoking, r/quittingvaping
- Never spam—provide genuine value first
- Share IFQ only when directly relevant and helpful
- Same proactive engagement approach: find struggling users, offer support
- AMA potential once product is proven

### Paid Acquisition

**Phase 1: Validation (0-1,000 users)**
- Minimal paid spend
- Small TikTok Spark Ads budget to boost top organic performers
- Goal: Validate messaging, not scale

**Phase 2: Amplification (1,000-10,000 users)**
- Scale TikTok ads with proven creative
- Instagram/Facebook retargeting
- Apple Search Ads on high-intent keywords
- Budget: 20-30% of MRR reinvested

**Phase 3: Scale (10,000+ users)**
- Diversified paid mix across platforms
- Influencer partnerships (see below)
- Podcast sponsorships in health/wellness space
- Budget: Based on LTV:CAC ratios

### Influencer Strategy

**Tier 1: Micro-influencers (1K-50K followers)**
- Health, fitness, lifestyle, "adulting" niches
- Authentic quit stories preferred
- Gifted access + affiliate commission
- Volume play: 20-50 partnerships

**Tier 2: Mid-tier (50K-500K followers)**
- Selective partnerships with strong audience fit
- Paid flat fee + performance bonus
- Longer-term ambassadorships preferred

**Tier 3: Macro (500K+ followers)**
- Reserved for proven product-market fit
- Must align with brand values
- No wellness gurus who've never struggled

**Partnership Criteria:**
- Authentic connection to quitting (personal experience preferred)
- Audience demo matches ICP
- Engagement rate > follower count
- Comfortable with IFQ's bold voice

## Growth

### Growth Stages

#### Stage 1: 0 → 100 Users (Validation)

**Goal:** Prove the product works and people will pay

**Tactics:**
- Personal network outreach (founder's contacts who smoke/vape)
- Direct recruitment from Reddit communities (genuine, not spammy)
- Hand-hold every user through onboarding
- Daily founder conversations with users
- Iterate rapidly based on feedback

**Success Criteria:**
- 50%+ users complete onboarding
- 20%+ convert to paid after trial
- 3+ users hit 30-day smoke-free
- Net Promoter Score > 50

#### Stage 2: 100 → 1,000 Users (Product-Market Fit)

**Goal:** Find repeatable acquisition channels, prove retention

**Tactics:**
- Launch organic TikTok content engine (daily posting)
- Launch Instagram presence
- First wave of micro-influencer partnerships (10-20)
- Implement referral program ("Invite a friend who wants to quit")
- Collect and publish success stories
- Small paid amplification on top performers

**Success Criteria:**
- Organic content driving 50%+ of signups
- Month-1 retention > 40%
- Paid conversion rate > 15%
- At least 2 channels showing scalable unit economics

#### Stage 3: 1,000 → 10,000 Users (Scale)

**Goal:** Pour fuel on what works, expand channel mix

**Tactics:**
- Scale winning TikTok/Instagram content formats
- Launch YouTube long-form content
- Expand influencer program (50+ partnerships)
- Increase paid spend on proven channels
- PR push: pitch quit success stories to health media
- Launch community features (shared milestones, group support)
- Explore partnerships (corporate wellness, health insurers)

**Success Criteria:**
- Sustainable CAC < 1/3 of LTV
- Month-3 retention > 30%
- Organic:Paid ratio of 60:40 or better
- Word-of-mouth/referral driving 20%+ of new users

#### Stage 4: 10,000+ Users (Dominance)

**Goal:** Become the default choice for quitting nicotine

**Tactics:**
- Brand campaigns (awareness over conversion)
- Major influencer/celebrity partnerships
- Podcast advertising at scale
- International expansion
- B2B channel (employers, insurers, health systems)
- Potential Android launch based on demand

### Metrics

**North Star Metric:** Users who reach 30-day smoke/vape-free

**Acquisition Metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| CAC | Cost to acquire a paying user | < $30 |
| Organic % | Signups from non-paid channels | > 50% |
| Referral Rate | Users who invite at least 1 friend | > 20% |
| App Store Conversion | Page view → Download | > 30% |

**Activation Metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Onboarding Completion | Users who complete full onboarding | > 70% |
| First Vera Interaction | Users who have meaningful first chat | > 90% |
| Trial → Paid | Free trial converts to subscription | > 20% |

**Retention Metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Day 7 Retention | Users active on day 7 | > 50% |
| Day 30 Retention | Users active on day 30 | > 35% |
| Month 3 Retention | Subscribers retained at month 3 | > 30% |
| Churn Rate | Monthly subscriber churn | < 15% |

**Engagement Metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Daily Active / Monthly Active | DAU/MAU ratio | > 40% |
| Messages per Day | Avg Vera interactions per active user | > 3 |
| Check-in Response Rate | Users responding to Vera check-ins | > 60% |

**Revenue Metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| LTV | Lifetime value per subscriber | > $100 |
| LTV:CAC | Ratio of lifetime value to acquisition cost | > 3:1 |
| MRR | Monthly recurring revenue | Track growth |
| ARPU | Average revenue per user | Track trend |

- Month 3: 500 users, product-market fit validated

- Month 12: 10,000 users, $50K MRR

**Success Stories:**

## Launch Plan

### Pre-Launch (4-6 weeks before)

**Product:**
- [ ] Beta testing with 20-50 users (friends, family, early recruits)
- [ ] Iterate based on feedback—especially onboarding and first-week experience
- [ ] Ensure Vera conversations feel natural and helpful
- [ ] Load test infrastructure

**App Store:**
- [ ] Prepare all ASO assets (screenshots, preview video, description)
- [ ] Set up App Store Connect, pricing, subscriptions
- [ ] Prepare for App Review (health app requirements, 17+ rating)

**Marketing:**
- [ ] Build pre-launch landing page with email capture
- [ ] Start TikTok/Instagram accounts, begin posting organic content
- [ ] Identify and reach out to first wave of micro-influencers
- [ ] Prepare launch day content (across all channels)
- [ ] Draft press release and media list

**Community:**
- [ ] Begin authentic engagement in Reddit quit communities
- [ ] Build small Discord/community for beta testers
- [ ] Document beta user stories for launch content

### Launch (Week of)

**Day -1:**
- [ ] Final App Store submission
- [ ] Prep all scheduled content
- [ ] Brief influencer partners on timing

**Launch Day:**
- [ ] App goes live on App Store
- [ ] Announce across all social channels
- [ ] Email blast to pre-launch list
- [ ] Influencer posts go live
- [ ] Founder posts personal story/announcement
- [ ] Press release distribution
- [ ] Monitor App Store for any issues

**Launch Week:**
- [ ] Daily content pushing the launch
- [ ] Respond to every comment, review, mention
- [ ] Real-time monitoring of onboarding completion rates
- [ ] Hotfix anything broken immediately
- [ ] Capture and reshare early user reactions

### Post-Launch (Ongoing)

**Week 1-2:**
- [ ] Intense user feedback collection
- [ ] Daily check on metrics vs. targets
- [ ] Rapid iteration on any friction points
- [ ] Double down on content that's performing

**Month 1:**
- [ ] First cohort retention analysis
- [ ] Identify top acquisition channels
- [ ] Collect first success stories
- [ ] Plan content calendar for Month 2
- [ ] Evaluate influencer partnership performance

**Month 2-3:**
- [ ] Scale what's working
- [ ] Kill what's not
- [ ] Launch referral program
- [ ] Begin paid amplification tests
- [ ] Expand influencer partnerships

**Ongoing:**
- [ ] Weekly metrics review
- [ ] Monthly strategy adjustment
- [ ] Quarterly major feature releases
- [ ] Continuous content engine operation
- [ ] Community building and engagement
