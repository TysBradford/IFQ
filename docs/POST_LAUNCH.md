# Post-Launch Feature Ideas

Features and concepts that won't make the MVP cut but are worth pursuing later.

---

## Community & Social Support

### Anonymous Peer Support

**Concept:** A community feature where users can anonymously encourage each other and provide support during their quit journey.

**Core elements:**
- Anonymous interactions (no real names, optional handles)
- Quick encouragement actions (reactions, short messages)
- Shared milestones and celebrations

### Support Pods

**Concept:** Dedicated small groups of X members who start the service around the same time, creating a cohort-based support system.

**How it could work:**
- Users matched into pods based on quit date proximity
- Private group space for the pod members
- Shared progress visibility within the pod
- Group challenges and collective milestones

**Moderation considerations:**
- LLM moderator in each group to filter harmful content
- Real-time content screening before messages appear
- Escalation path for flagged content
- Clear community guidelines enforced automatically

**Open questions:**
- Optimal pod size (5-10 members?)
- How to handle inactive or dropped members
- Balance between anonymity and accountability
- Integration with Vera (could she facilitate pod discussions?)

### Accountability Partners

**Concept:** Pair up with someone you know (or get matched) to quit together. You see each other's progress, get nudged when your partner is struggling, and celebrate wins together. The social contract of not wanting to let someone else down is one of the strongest motivators there is.

**How it could work:**
- Invite a friend via link/code, or opt into matching with a stranger on a similar quit timeline
- Shared dashboard: both partners see each other's streak, milestones, and recent activity
- Nudge system: if your partner hasn't checked in, you get a prompt to send encouragement
- Mutual alerts: optional notification when your partner logs a craving (so you can reach out)
- Joint milestones: "You and Sam both hit 30 days" — celebrate together
- Vera can facilitate: "Your partner just hit a rough patch. Want to send them a message?"

**Why this matters for retention:**
- Social commitment is a proven behaviour change lever — people are more likely to stick with something when someone else is counting on them
- Creates a reason to open the app even on easy days (checking in on your partner)
- Natural referral mechanism — every accountability partner is a new user
- Emotional investment in another person's journey makes churning feel like abandoning someone

**Privacy considerations:**
- Users control what's shared (streak only, or full activity)
- Either partner can disconnect at any time with no guilt messaging
- Anonymous matching pairs don't exchange real names unless they choose to
- Vera mediates if one partner goes silent (checks in privately before alerting the other)

**Open questions:**
- 1:1 pairs vs small groups (2-3 people)?
- How to handle it gracefully when one partner relapses or goes inactive
- Should partners be able to chat directly, or only through structured interactions?
- Free feature or premium upsell?
