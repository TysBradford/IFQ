## The Core Tension

We need to walk the line between:
- **"Larger than life"** - matching the brand's rebellious, intense energy
- **Not aggravating** - still feeling trustworthy, usable, not exhausting

The brand is called "I Fucking Quit" - it needs to feel bold. But people using this app are going through something hard. The UI can't add to their stress.

---

## Controlled Chaos (Key Insight)

**The solution isn't consistent energy - it's rhythmic intensity.**

Instead of every section being equally bold/aggressive:
- Calm, spacious sections for content-heavy areas (reading, forms, information)
- Explosive visual moments for statements, celebrations, key brand moments

The energy comes in **bursts**, not constant assault.

Think of it like music - you need the quiet verse to make the chorus hit harder.

### Application:
- Hero section: HIGH energy (this is the brand statement)
- Features/details: CALM (people need to read and understand)
- Statement sections: HIGH energy (punctuation moments)
- Vera section: WARM, approachable (she's the supportive friend)
- CTA: HIGH energy (action moment)

---

## Texture = Warmth

Bold colors can feel cold/digital/startup-y.

Adding subtle texture (paper grain, noise) makes the same palette feel:
- More human
- More tactile
- Less "tech company landing page"
- Warmer without muting the colors

---

## Color Direction: Emerald

Electric blue reads "tech startup" - been done to death.

Deep emerald (#00A878 or similar) offers:
- Still bold and punchy
- More sophisticated
- Unexpected for this category
- Works well with paper/cream base
- Pairs well with warm gold/amber for celebrations

---

## Typography as Hero

If color becomes overwhelming, let typography carry the boldness:
- Extreme size contrasts (small labels vs massive headlines)
- Weight mixing within headlines (light + ultra bold)
- Near-monochrome palette
- The rebellion comes from TYPE, not screaming colors

---

## Split Palette

Use light/dark strategically:
- Light mode: content-heavy sections (readable, trustworthy)
- Dark mode: statement moments (dramatic, impactful)

The contrast between sections creates visual rhythm and breathing room.
